CBDCs & Policy

Governments aren't ignoring digital currency. They're building their own. Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) are being developed worldwide. Understanding the difference between CBDCs and Bitcoin is critical.

~20–30 min of content

The Surveillance Currency

A CBDC gives a central bank direct visibility and control over every transaction you make. They can programme your money to expire, restrict what you buy, or freeze your account instantly. This is not a conspiracy theory. These are stated design features. Bitcoin is the opposite: permissionless, borderless, and controlled by no single entity.

Debunking the FUD

You'll hear that quantum computing will "break" Bitcoin. This is the new Y2K. Bitcoin's cryptography can be upgraded. The network has survived every technical and political attack thrown at it for 15 years. Quantum is a known variable, not an unknown threat, and the Bitcoin developer community has been preparing for it since 2016.

Buy, Store, HODL

A practical guide to buying your first Bitcoin, securing it properly, and building a long-term strategy. Australian-focused with global options.

How to Buy Bitcoin

The most straightforward way to buy Bitcoin is through a reputable Bitcoin-only exchange. These platforms focus exclusively on Bitcoin, reducing distractions and risks associated with altcoins. Globally, the most trusted Bitcoin-only exchanges include Swan Bitcoin, River, and Bull Bitcoin.

If you're in Australia, you have excellent local options. Bitaroo is a user-friendly, high-security exchange for buying and selling Bitcoin with AUD, offering advanced trading tools and Lightning Network integration. Amber is a mobile-first platform designed for easy dollar-cost averaging (DCA), supporting Lightning payments and ideal for beginners. Hardblock is another trusted exchange, known for its simple interface and auto-DCA feature.

To get started, create an account on one of these platforms and complete the setup process. Once your account is funded, you can buy Bitcoin. Always withdraw your Bitcoin to a personal wallet you control after your purchase.

Hot Wallets vs. Cold Wallets

Once you've purchased Bitcoin, the next step is to move it off the exchange and into a wallet you control. This is crucial for your security and privacy.

A hot wallet is a software wallet connected to the internet, making it convenient for everyday use and small amounts. Think of it like a physical wallet in your pocket. One of the best hot wallets for beginners is BlueWallet: open-source, easy to use, supports both on-chain and Lightning transactions, available on iOS and Android.

For larger amounts or long-term storage, a cold wallet (hardware wallet) is the gold standard. Cold wallets keep your private keys offline and safe from online threats. Coldcard is a highly respected Bitcoin-only hardware wallet known for robust security and open-source firmware. The BitBox02 (Bitcoin-only edition) is another excellent choice, offering a simple, secure, privacy-focused experience.

Why Withdraw from Exchanges?

Leaving your Bitcoin on an exchange means you don't truly own it. The exchange holds the private keys, and you're exposed to risks like hacks, insolvency, or frozen accounts.

By withdrawing to your own wallet, you take full control of your Bitcoin, ensuring that only you can access or move your funds. This is the essence of "not your keys, not your coins."
